Indonesian Spicy Penyet Chicken

Penyet chicken, is a dish originated from East Java, Indonesia. The name penyet is actually Javanese for "pressed", which refers to the process where the chicken is pressed with a pestle to tenderize it.

Penyet chicken offers a range of texture and flavors, from the crispy chicken skin and the tenderness of its meat seasoned with all kinds of spices, to its aromatic sambal spiciness.

This mouthwatering delicacies often accompanied with fresh tasting greens, which refreshes your palate, making you go craving for more! Without further ado, here are the recipe that will fulfill your chicken craving.

Ingredients

Penyet Chicken

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into 4 or 8 pcs
  • 1 chicken stock cube
  • 1 inch of ginger
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • water

Marination

  • 1/2 spoon of sweet cumin
  • 1/2 spoon of white cumin
  • 3 tablespoons of coriander
  • 1 tablespoons of tumeric powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon of white pepper powder
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1 inch of ginger
  • salt to taste

Batter

  • extra marination ingredients
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup of rice flour

Rice

  • 3 cups of rice, washed and drained
  • 6 cups of boiled chicken water
  • 3 cloves of garlic, smashed
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 star anises
  • 5 cardamom seeds
  • 1 spoon of butter or ghee
  • 1 pandan leaf, shredded and knotted
  • salt to taste

Sambal

  • 10 chilli padis
  • 10 red chillis
  • 10 red onions, cut into 4
  • 2 cloves of garlic, cut coarsely
  • 3 tomatoes, cut into 4
  • 5cm of belacan bakar
  • 1/2 handful of anchovies
  • 3 tablespoons of gula melaka, or to taste
  • 1 cup of oil
  • lime
  • salt to taste

(Optional) Accompaniments

  • 4 pieces of tofu
  • 2 pieces of tempeh
  • an assortment of vegetables (cucumber, salam leaf, cabbage, long beans, tomato)

Step by Step Directions

Chicken Preparation

  1. Clean the chicken and boil it with ginger, garlic and the chicken stock cube for approximately 15 minutes or until cooked.
  2. Remove the chicken and set it aside.
  3. Save the water for the rice and proceed to the next step.

Marination Process

  1. Fry sweet cumin, white cumin, and coriander on a non-oiled pan until fragrant.
  2. Allow spices to cool, then grind them with the rest of marination ingridients until smooth.
  3. Marinate previously boiled chicken for a minimum of 2 hours, allowing the chicken to absorb spices.
  4. Fry the chiken until crisp. (Before frying, you can also smash or press the chicken to tenderize it)
  5. Marinate tofu and tempeh separately from the chicken and set them aside.
  6. Use the extra marinade for the batter and proceed to the next step.

Batter Preparation

  1. Mix the extra marinade with rice, flour and egg.
  2. Slowly, pour this mixture into the pan and fry until crisp. (Alternatively you can also coat the chicken in batter then fry it)
  3. Batter is ready and can be used as dish condiments.

Rice Preparation

  1. Clean sautee butter, garlic, cinnamon, star anises, cardamom and pandan until fragrant.
  2. Add rice and stir.
  3. Add water and a little salt.
  4. Close the lid and let it cook in the pot or rice cooker.

Sambal Preparation

  1. Fry anchovies until crisp and set them aside.
  2. Fry chillies and red onion in the same oil then fry tomatoes until soft.
  3. Roughly grind anchovies, chillies, red onion, tomatoes and belacan bakar.
  4. Heat extra oil and cook sambal in it with gula melaka.
  5. Season with salt to taste and sambal is ready.

Accompaniments Preparation

  1. Fry previously marinated tofu and tempeh until crisp.
  2. Clean and slice vegetables.

Serve Chicken with Rice, Sambal, Batter, and Accompaniments on a Plate and Enjoy!
